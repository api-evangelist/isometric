This article examines how SBTi's Corporate Net Zero Standard 2.0 will reshape carbon credit demand, highlighting the new Ongoing Emissions Responsibility (OER) program with three recognition tiers — Engaged, Advanced, and Leadership — that require companies to take responsibility for progressively larger shares of their emissions. It projects meaningful near-term demand for reductions and nature-based removals around $20-80 per tonne, while engineered carbon removal must wait until mandatory requirements begin in 2035, when companies must offset 1% of ongoing emissions with durable removal credits, scaling upward over time.
